### Java Champions are leaders.

Sharat Chander has spent over two decades building and nurturing the global Java community. 

As Oracle's former Senior Director of Java Developer Relations and Head of Developer Community Engagement, he served as JavaOne Conference Content Chairperson for over 10 years, led multiple initiatives at Oracle for the Java community, including, Java's major milestone celebrations, Java releases, and conferences among many others. He has presented as a keynote speaker and community leader at popular Java conferences (JavaOne, Devnexus, Jfokus, Devoxx, JFall, JavaZone, JCON, GIDS, etc.), and several Java User Groups around the world. 

Sharat has shaped both the Java community and the strategy around it, all the way from the Sun Microsystems era through Oracle’s stewardship of Java. He has made a positive impact on millions of developers worldwide through authentic advocacy, educational initiatives, and tireless support of the Java community globally.

His passion for the Java community went far beyond employment obligations. He led these initiatives with genuine care and personal investment, and that's exactly why they succeeded.

Sharat steered the rejuvenation of JavaOne after a multi-year hiatus. He has been the essential host, organizer and a key speaker at the conference for several years starting all the way from 2011, until 2025. As a long-time content chair of JavaOne, he shaped the technical agenda and themes, ensured strong community participation and brought together Java developers from around the world.

He has led Java’s milestone campaigns, such as Java’s 25th (#MovedByJava) and 30th anniversary (#JavaAt30).
External to JavaOne, Sharat has actively spearheaded the creation of Java User Groups, connecting speakers to the JUGs, speaking at milestone JUG events and ensuring that the Java User Groups stay active.

Sharat has even traveled on his personal dime to speak at JUGs. He has spoken at over a 100 JUGs fostering community and building up the Java Community over the years.
 
Sharat is the Sherpa of the Java Community.

### Java Champions are involved in innovative applications of Java technology, humanitarian efforts, or educational programs.

Sharat found creative ways to bring the community together. At JavaOne 2025, during Java's 30th anniversary, he co-led a time capsule initiative where developers from around the world could share their visions for the next decade of Java. It's a small gesture in some ways, but it reinforces something important: this platform belongs to all of us, and everyone's voice matters in shaping where it goes next.

During the Sun Microsystems era, he organized Sun Tech Days, which was a global initiative that brought Java education to emerging markets across Asia, Latin America, Africa, and Eastern Europe. Years later, for Java's 25th anniversary (2020-2021), he put together programs that included a special $25 certification exam, free training, and a global webcast series that gave community members a platform to share their stories. These initiatives show educational democratization. 

What really stands out is the human impact. On the Duke's Corner podcast, Sharat shared stories from developers around the world: "Java saved my life," "Java took me out of poverty," "Java funded my college education." These aren't just feel-good anecdotes, they represent real people whose lives changed because someone invested in bringing Java education to their communities.
